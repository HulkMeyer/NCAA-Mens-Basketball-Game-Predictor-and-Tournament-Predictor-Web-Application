Christopher Colt Meyer

Educator | Data Scientist | Army Officer | Father

Where Have I Been

Born and raised in Lawton, Oklahoma, I graduated from MacArthur High School where I played football and track, earning All-Star by class honors. I continued my athletic and academic journey at Wayne State College, where I was a 4-year starter on the football team and earned a Bachelor’s degree in Mathematics Education.

I pursued graduate studies twice, completing a Master of Science in Organizational Leadership from Cameron University and coursework toward a Master of Science in Mathematics at the University of Nebraska at Omaha. Alongside academics, I played semi-pro football for the Omaha Nighthawks and became a state and national record holder in powerlifting (best combined total: Squat 640, Bench 420, Deadlift 615, Total 1675 lbs). I’ve also tested my endurance by completing six Tough Mudder events.

Where Am I Now

I am a proud husband and father of five: John (21), Colt (16), Keeley (10), Taylor (9), and Evan (8). Our home is lively with six dogs (Mac, Bailey, Husker, Panda, Max, Bluey) and one cat (Yankee).

Professionally, I am a Pre-Engineering Instructor at Great Plains Technology Center, and I am completing my Master of Science in Data Science at Eastern University (graduating December). I also serve as a Captain in the Oklahoma Army National Guard, with over 12 years of service.
